Adiction awarenes installation

The goal of this installation was to show young adults the consequences of two common addictions: smoking and drinking. Many young people engage in these behaviors without understanding their long-term effects on important organs such as the heart, brain, liver, and lungs. When entering the installation, the user walks into an operating room with a human body on an operating table. The body is open so the organs can be seen. The organs were made using life-size latex balloons connected to a pneumatic system, which allowed the heart to beat and the lungs to inflate. LED strips were used to show color changes representing damage. On a nearby table, there are objects representing different levels of addiction, such as bottles and packs of cigarettes. The user can pick up one of these objects to see how the addiction affects the body over time. For example, alcohol causes the heart to grow, the liver to enlarge and change color, and the brain to shrink. These effects continue until the body dies from excessive consumption. The strength of the effects depends on the substance and the level chosen by the user. The room also includes 360-degree audio and a screen that shows additional information. Users can also interact with a puzzle representing the organs. If a piece, such as the liver, is removed from the puzzle, that organ will disappear from the body display.
