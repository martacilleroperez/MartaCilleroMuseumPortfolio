Real time feedback on overstriding for runners

Overstriding is one of the most common issues in runners nowadays, especially in amateur runners. This happens when a runner’s foot lands heel-first well in front of the knee. After talking with experts as well as doing literature research we developed a prototype. This consists of exoskeleton with rotary joints, with potentiometers used to measure the angles of the lower limbs. Based on these angles it is determined whether the runner is overstriding. Then for the feedback, there is a bracelet consisting of a vibration motor that gives the user haptic feedback during their run. The vibration works as a metronome to guide the user's step rate.
